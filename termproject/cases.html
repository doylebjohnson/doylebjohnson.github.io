Sample Litigation Matters:

  • Advanced Interactive v. Matsushita, et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving data broadcasting over a television signal. Obtained judgment of non-infringement which was affirmed by the Federal Circuit.
  • ArrayComm v. Atheros Communication Represented defendant in patent infringement action in Eastern District of Texas involving Wi-Fi technology.
  • Atheros Communications v. CSIRO Represented declaratory judgment plaintiff in patent infringement action involving Wi-Fi technology.
  • Auditory Licensing v. Siemens Medical Represented defendant in patent matter concerning “open ear” configurations for hearing aids.
  • Centripetal Networks v. Keysight Technologies Represent defendant in patent infringement suit involving network security appliances.
  • Concinnitas v. NETGEAR Represented defendant in a patent infringement lawsuit involving mobile wireless technology.
  • Crossroads v. Tandberg Represented defendant in patent infringement matter involving network attached tape library systems.
  • Data Network Storage v. D-Link and NETGEAR Represented defendants in patent infringement matter concerning iSCSI network storage devices (SCSI over IP).
  • Data Retrieval v. Sybase and Informatica Represented defendants in a patent infringement action involving database modeling software.
  • DJO v. CoolSystems Represented defendant in patent infringement case involving medical cooling device.
  • DR Systems v. Siemens, et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving medical imaging database technology.
  • Ericsson v. D-Link, NETGEAR, Acer, et al. Represented defendants in patent infringement action relating to wireless communications technologies (Wi-Fi), including complex ARQ (Automatic Repeat Request) protocol issues.
  • EZ4Media v. NETGEAR et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to streaming media player technology.
  • Freeny v. NETGEAR Represented defendant in patent infringement matter involving dual-band Wi-Fi router technology.
  • Fujitsu, Philips, and LG v. NETGEAR Represented third-party defendant in patent infringement case relating to wireless technology.
  • Juxtacomm v. TIBCO et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement case relating to data format conversion software. Obtained summary judgment of invalidity.
  • Hedgecoth v. Seagate Represented plaintiff in patent infringement actions involving hard disk drive technology against several defendants, resulting in favorable settlements.
  • Advanced Dynamic Interfaces LLC v. IronSpeed Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving technology for automatic code generation for database applications.
  • Lonestar v. Atheros Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving capacitor technology.
  • MPH Technologies v. Check Point Software et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement case involving VPN and NAT translation tunneling technology.
  • Network-1 v. D-Link Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to "Power over Ethernet" (PoE) technology.
  • Network Congestion Solutions v. Century Link et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving network congestion control. Located “killer” prior art and filed an Inter Partes Review (IPR) action at the USPTO, which resulted in a favorable settlement for many of the defendants in the case.
  • Northrop Grumman v. Siemens Medical Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to hearing aid signal processing technology.
  • Open Text v. Box Represented defendant in patent infringement suit involving 12 patents relating to Groupware, file synchronization, and action notification. Located key prior art software products, many of which were almost 20 years old, and created video clips of the old software in operation. Many patents were invalidated and cases settled after trial and before appeals were finalized.
  • Powerline v. Qualcomm Atheros et al. Represented defendants in a patent infringement action involving home powerline networking products.
  • Protegrity v. Informatica Representing defendant in patent infringement case involving database element encryption and data masking technologies.
  • Red River Fiber Optic Corp. v. Qwest Communications Corp. et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to fiber optic telecommunications networks.
  • Seiko Epson v. Multi-Union Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to ink jet print cartridge technology
  • Serious Materials v. Supress Products Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving acoustical building materials.
  • Siemens v. Kromek Represented plaintiff in patent infringement action concerning X-ray image sensors.
  • Sportsvision v. SportsMEDIA Technology Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to the first down line (i.e. “yellow line”) in football game broadcasts.
  • STC.UNM v. Dell Represent defendant in patent infringement matter involving IEEE 802.11ac WiFI products.
  • Stratos v. Infineon Represented defendant in patent infringement lawsuit relating to optoelectronic transceivers.
  • Sunpower v. SunLink Represented defendant in patent infringement case involving photovoltaic panel roof mounting systems.
  • Sybase v. Vertica Represented plaintiff in patent infringement action involving columnar database technology.
  • Sycamore IP Holdings LLC v. CenturyLink Represent telecommunications defendant in a patent infringement case involving generic framing procedure (GFP) and related optical encoding protocols.
  • Uniloc v. Box Represent defendant Box in patent infringement cases involving software download and update technology.
  • USEI v. Atheros et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving Ethernet controller chips.
  • The PACid Group v. Atheros et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving Wi-Fi encryption technology.
  • TR Labs v. Century Link et al. Represented defendant in patent infringement action involving optical mesh networking technology.
  • University of Nebraska v. Siemens Healthcare Diagnostics Represented defendant in patent infringement action relating to automated lab processing systems. Obtained summary judgment of non-infringement.
  • Wi-LAN v. Atheros et al. Represented defendants in patent infringement action in Eastern District of Texas involving Wi-Fi and DSL technology.
  • X-One v. Uber Represent defendant Uber in patent infringement case involving two-way tracking of a service provider and requestor.
  • YYZ v. BMC Represented defendant in patent case involving process monitoring and tracking for mainframe software.
